About Game

What is our game?

Developed by Aston Computer Entertainment (ACE), Anya and Zuzannah is a turn based role playing game targeted at the age range of 9-12. Anya, a Russian scientist searching for artefacts in Angel Falls, Venezuela finds an ancient rock made of mankind's rarest material; unobtanium; with properties that enhances the physical abilities of the wielder. Anya attracts the attention of “The Bandidos Del Caos”, who seek the unobtanium for their own purposes. Alongside Zuzannah, a rebel with a grudge against the Bandidos, Anya must fight against bandit and wildlife alike to escape Angel Falls and to deliver the unobtanium to Gemini Corporation, who promise a handsome award. As Anya progresses throughout the adventure, she'll discover the true motives of those around her, as well as learn about the origin of the ubobtanium. Have you got what it takes?

Features of Game

Hand drawn graphics

With hand drawn graphics, Anya and Zuzannah's distinct visual style is sure to win you over!

Android Platform

Developed from the ground up for Android, Anya and Zuzannah is designed for both long playthroughs and "short breaks at works" periods.

Turn Based RPG

Our game incorporates a turn based strategy engine, allowing a range of different moves and exciting situations to unfold.

System Requirements

Android 4.0+

Android Version 4.0 (Ice Cream Sandwich)+ is required to play our game. Android 6.0+ is recommended.

Size

Our game requires at least 200MB of storage. 1GB+ is recommended.

Memory

Our game requires 1GB RAM. More RAM is preferable.

Blog Introduction

November 10, 2019

As part of the game’s development, Regular updates will be provided on the game as we progress throughout development. As part of the first update, one of our milestones was… making a website! This blog hosts our updates so you can keep up to date with what we’ve been doing.

So what have we been doing? We’ve been quite busy but before the deadline we managed to get this done:

Game Design Document: Our design document describes the game, our audience and our overall vision for the game, as well as serving as a reference point throughout the game’s development.

Software Quality Strategy: The quality strategy document evaluates what design methodologies we are using to develop the project (i.e. agile development or the waterfall model), and what tools we are using to assist our development (i.e. Trello for keeping track of tasks).

Website: This website hosts our blog (updates to our game), as well as a download link to our game.

Game engine: Java and libgdx – used as we have programmers on the team already familiar with Java, and two members learnt how to use libgdx.

Last but not least, a basic prototype of the actual game! This is intended as a quick hack just to demonstrate the feasibility of our ideas and if the game works+ is fun to play in practice.

After some constructive feedback we’ve managed to flesh out the game’s story, and we’re looking forward to really getting into developing the game further. Until then, stay tuned!
